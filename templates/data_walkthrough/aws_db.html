Mapping Campaign Financiers

Drawing on publicly available FEC Campaign Contribution datasets from the 2016 Election cycle and ACS 5-Year Census estimates at the zip code level, this project uses machine learning techniques to identify how campaign finance influences elections.
An immediate application, political groups can quickly analyze from where donations were secured during the 2016 to better target potential donors during future campaigns. This may also serve as a way to begin to map how inside and outside political money influences elections at the local level.
In terms of scope, our dataset includes donations of $200 or greater, given directly to party committees. This only scratches the surface of on type of revenue, among the vast array of "grey money" tactics currently at play in US elections (e.g. donations given to PACs, or to related nonprofit organizations, or to politically motivated private organizations).
